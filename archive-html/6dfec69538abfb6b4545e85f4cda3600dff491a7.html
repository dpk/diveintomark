(advertising, apple, css, jaguar, linkdump, muse, osx, privacy, privoxy, rdf, rss, webservices)

More on RSS:

  • Dave Winer: Discontinuities. So it’s in this context that I ask why should we “deprecate” some elements of RSS 2.0.
  • Sam Ruby: Re: Discontinuities. I dare say that RSS would not be the success it is today without these discontinuities. In particular, making title optional was a bold step that broke a number of assumptions that tools were making but made available a large number of feeds.
  • Shelley Powers: RSS and disappointment. I’m disappointed because assumptions have been made that the efforts of the RSS 1.0 working group and Userland can never merge.
  • Jon Hanna: Re: Time to move along? – Make hard decisions cleanly. I’m not going to be enthused by a non-RDF syndication format. … I think RSS1.0 got the balance pretty much spot-on in supporting both RDF and XML views, but if you disagree I advise you not to try to placate us. Abandon us completely!
  • Kevin Burton: The end of RSS innocence. We need to move on. The innocence is over. RSS is going to be an RDF application with rich and complex modules that include RDF vocabulary. In short RSS is going to become complicated.
  • Kevin Burton: The RSS 0.9x branch is just a temporary diversion while people are still trying to grasp the semantic web. I agree 100%. Keep in mind, however, that HTML is just a temporary diversion while people are still trying to grasp SGML. (See also: The Rise of Worse is Better)
  • Phil Ringnalda: RDF: Not for the faint of heart. Then I pointed rdfdump at my XHMTL page, to see what the TrackBack RDF would look like. Uh oh, judging by the error it throws, the RDF parser is yet another thing that doesn’t approve of RDF embedded in XHTML. So unless I’m missing something, I’ll have to parse out the RDF, and then parse it (which I’d probably have to do anyway, what with people commenting it out rather than use cloaking to hide it from validators). Sigh. I can’t help thinking how easy it would be to add mod_trackback to RSS 0.94/2.0, and just parse the XML as XML.
  • Joe Gregorio: Obvious Utility. I do not have a problem for which RDF is a solution.
  • The Road to RSS 3.0. He is, of course, kidding. At least I hope he’s kidding. If he’s not kidding, we should rename it Plain Text Syndication and write it up as an RFC. (More comments here.)
