Life Assessment Quiz

(personal, quiz)

Everyone over the age of 25 should stop whatever they’re doing and take this quiz. It’s not for teenagers, and it’s not for LiveJournal, and you won’t get a cute little graphic or a tagline when you’re done. You will however get a score, and a lot to think about. My score is 77, and here’s what I’m thinking about:

Physical environment:

  • My bed is rarely made.
  • My clothes are rarely clean, ironed, or put away.
  • I do not consistently have adequate time in my life.
  • I am not consistently on time. Time management has never been my strong point.
  • I do not drink enough water. I have had two bladder infections and one kidney infection in the past five years stemming from dehydration.

Well-being:

  • I eat too much sugar.
  • I have not seen a dentist in three years, although the day before discovering this quiz, I called to make an appointment.
  • I have not had an eye exam in three years, although I signed up for the vision plan that my new employer offers.
  • My weight is not within my ideal range, although it is within 5 pounds of it.

Money:

  • I do not have a financial plan for the next year, although I have made financial plans in previous years.
  • I have missed several days of work due to illness in the past few months.

Relationships:

  • I have not let go of relationships which drag me down or damage me. This is my number one priority for the next six months.
  • I rarely put people first and results second.
  • I have not attempted to communicate with everyone I have hurt.
  • I have not forgiven those who have hurt me.
  • There are several people I would feel uncomfortable randomly bumping into.
  • I frequently gossip.
  • I rarely tell people how they can satisfy me.
  • I am rarely caught up with letters, calls, and e-mail.
  • I do not always tell the truth no matter what.
  • I sometimes promise things and don’t deliver them.
  • I frequently judge and criticize others.
  • I frequently take things personally.

Obviously, the “relationships” category is my weakest point. This should not come as a raging shock to anyone, but it’s somewhat depressing to have it confirmed by a random quiz on the internet. As I said, my number one priority for the next six months is letting go of some attachments that are dragging me down. I’ll try to give you a progress report on that in a month or so, although keep in mind that I sometimes promise things and don’t deliver them, so don’t hold your breath.

What’s your number one priority from this list?
