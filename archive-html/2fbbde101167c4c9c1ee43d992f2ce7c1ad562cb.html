‣ May 15, 2003 ‣

Pre-wedding dinner

(wedding)

These are very special people.

  • Michael and his son Alex, his wife Christine and daughter Sarah
  • My Aunt Thelma and Uncle Jim, my father’s brother
  • My Uncle Don and Aunt Elizabeth, my mother’s sister
  • Joan, a long-time family friend
  • My second family, Roger and his wife Judith, their daughters Jill and Rachel, and her son Thomas

Judith gave us heart-shaped rings to keep in our pockets during the ceremony. Christine gave us a gift-wrapped bottle of sparkling cider. And Michael gave us The Grinch Who Stole Christmas in Latin, which is exactly the sort of wedding present I would expect from him. He also typed out and gave me his answers to my timeline questions:

  • 1987: Who the hell are you?
  • 1988: OK, but you’ll have to juggle your own balls. You’re not touching mine.
  • 1989: Why, are you already tired of juggling your own balls?
  • 1990: Who the hell is Canadian Brass?
  • 1991: Sure, what could be so difficult about that?
  • 1992: No problem. Do you think I should mention the nitrogen tri-iodide incident?
  • 1993: Sure, but… um… there are no waterbeds involved this time, are there?
  • 1996: I moved in with someone, but we’re not dating.
  • 1997: OK, we’re dating, but we’re not sleeping together.
  • 1998: Sure. Would your parents give me the house when they’re done with it?
  • 1998: Well, you take the thing, drape it over the other things, with the stuff on the side, and… oh, hell, just hold the damn stick, will you?
  • 1998: OK, OK, so maybe we did sleep together.
  • 1999: OK, but don’t go too much further.
  • 1999: Sure. What could go wrong?
  • 2000: Sure, someone’s got to be. And it’ll make the story that much more interesting for our grandkids.
  • 2000: Who the hell is Dora?
  • 2001: To North Carolina?! Fuck no.
  • 2002: It’d be the highest honor I could have.

And here we are.
