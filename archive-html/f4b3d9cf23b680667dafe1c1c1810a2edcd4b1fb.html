StarOffice 6.0 beta is out. [via Slashdot] This is a free Office clone. It is available in English and German for Windows, Linux/i386, and Solaris/Sparc. I downloaded the English Windows version. Initial impressions:

  • It’s large as downloads go (96 MB), but much much smaller than Microsoft Office. And yes, I have legitimately had to download Microsoft Office (my company’s site-licensed version, from our file server, through our VPN) to re-install it when things went horribly wrong one day.
  • The interface is slick. Much, much nicer than StarOffice 5.2, which lamely attempted to take over your entire desktop and keep you within StarOffice. Each application is now separate, but it’s definitely an integrated application suite, just like MS Office. File/New gives you a submenu to create a new document of any type (word processing, HTML, spreadsheet, presentation, drawing, and so forth).
  • It happily co-exists alongside Microsoft Office 2000. It doesn’t attempt to take over your file associations; double-clicking on your Word files will still open Microsoft Word. It does, however, install a QuickStart application that sits in your system tray and gives you File/New and Open options. A non-pixel-hogging version of the Microsoft Office bar. Slick.
  • It comes with a buttload of filters that no one will ever use. Let’s be honest, the only filters that matter are Microsoft Word, Excel, and Powerpoint. I have successfully opened all the Microsoft Word documents I have at my disposal (mostly functional specs given to us by clients, and technical specs written in-house). These are not the most complex Word documents in the world, but they have headers, footers, outlines, auto-numbered lists, embedded pictures, tables, and highlighted revision-controlled changes. All displayed without a hitch, edited without a hitch, and I was able to re-save the document with a few changes and then open it in Microsoft Word 2000. I don’t have any Excel spreadsheets to test at the moment, but given my experience with Word documents, I wouldn’t expect any problems.
  • To ease migration within your company (and to keep you compatible with the vast hordes of unenlightened masses outside your company), you can set StarOffice to automatically save in Microsoft Word/Excel/Powerpoint 2000 (or 97) format. Go to Tools/Options, then select Load/Save, then set your default document format for each type of document.

Overall, I’m quite impressed. For what I use Office for, this could easily replace The Real Thing. I wonder how many other people will come to the same conclusion.

Every time I write about the impossibility of effectively protecting digital files on a general-purpose computer, I get responses from people decrying the death of copyright. “How will authors and artists get paid for their work?” they ask me. Truth be told, I don’t know. I feel rather like the physicist who just explained relativity to a group of would-be interstellar travelers, only to be asked: “How do you expect us to get to the stars, then?” I’m sorry, but I don’t know that, either.
