The Ws of weblogging

(blogging, pie)

All the action is here: Anatomy of a Well-Formed Log Entry. (Original weblog post from Sam Ruby.) A distillation of all that we’ve learned about the technical aspects of this publishing genre.

My thoughts:

  • Permalinks are essential. They are the where of weblogging. They enable cross-site conversations through linking, the fundamental activity on the web. Note that permalinks don’t always map to individual pages. Some publishing systems (Movable Type, LiveJournal) default to page == permalink. Others (Radio, Blogger) default to page == date range, with multiple permalinks per page. But both are equally linkable, which is what matters.
  • Dates are essential. Obviously, they are the when of weblogging. When matters. It’s great that online publishing is so freeform, but it also means we need to create our own context, since so little is provided by default. Making dates visible establishes important context. People stumble onto old content all the time; referrals from search engines constitute over 50% of my daily hits. (This does not mean that every collection of posts needs to be arranged or displayed in reverse chronological order.)
  • Authors are essential. Visible authorship is the who of weblogging. Weblogging is about voice: finding your own, and recognizing others. Sign your name on everything you write. Even if it’s a pseudonym, sign your invented name on everything you write. Build an identity. (Of course, a single weblog can have more than one author. Boing Boing is a collective weblog, with an evolving collection of authors. Each post is signed by its individual author.)
  • MIME types are essential. They are the what of weblogging. Weblogging is on the web, and on the web we distinguish content by MIME types. Some people post HTML, or images, or audio, or even video. Some people apparently think entirely in XML. More power to them. Every piece of content has a MIME type; get it right.

More thoughts from Shelley Powers, Tim Bray, Joe Gregorio, Norman Walsh.
